The Salem Witch Trials

{{"what were the salem witch trials?" | title}}

The Salem Witch Trials were a series of investigations that resulted in the hangings of 19 "witches" and the imprisonment of countless other.

Parris' daughter Betty would not wake up one morning and Salem's doctor could not figure out why. He pronounced it "The work of the Devil." The night before, Parris had discovered his daughter, niece, and a few other girls, dancing in the forest with his slave, Tituba. Parris sent for the Minister of a nearby town, Reverend John Hale, who had previous experience with "witches". When Hale arrived, he examined Betty Parris and confimed that she was bewitched. The girls dancing with Tituba was mentioned and Reverend Hale interrogated Tituba.

Tituba's Interrogation

Reverend Hale asked Tituba if she was in service of the Devil. He told her he was able to help her and bring her back to God. Titbua confessed that she was a witch.
"Have you seen others with the Devil when he comes to you?" Hale asked Tituba.
At first, Tituba said she had not seen anyone. But when Hale asked the question again, she replied that yes she had seen others with the Devil.

The Accusing

During the examination of Betty and the interrogation of Tituba, Abigail and some of the other girls who had danced were also in the room. As soon as Tituba said she had seen other women, namely Sarah Good and Sarah Osborne. As soon as Tituba accused those women as witches, the girls started shouting that they had been bewitched those women. The women of course, denied any connection to the Devil. Abigial, the ringleader of the group started to accuse whichever of the women she didn't like.
